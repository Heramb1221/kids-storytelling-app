The Bear Who Forgot How to Hug
Once upon a time in the whispering woods, there lived a bear named Barnaby. Barnaby was known far and wide for giving the very best bear hugs. His hugs were warm, soft, and made everyone feel instantly happy. But one chilly morning, Barnaby woke up and realized he had completely forgotten how to hug.

He tried wrapping his arms around a tall pine tree, but he just ended up scratching his nose on the rough bark. He tried squeezing a big boulder by the river, but it was much too cold and hard. Barnaby sat down on a wooden stump and let out a very sad, rumbly sigh. How could he be a proper bear without knowing how to hug?

Just then, his best friend, a tiny squirrel named Squeak, hurried over with a paw full of shiny acorns. Squeak saw his friend looking miserable and dropped his acorns in the grass immediately. He asked Barnaby what was wrong, and the big bear explained his terrible, hug-less problem.

Squeak did not laugh. Instead, he walked right up to Barnaby's giant, furry tummy, threw his little squirrel arms as wide as they could possibly go, and squeezed with all his might. It was a very small hug, but it was full of kindness.

Barnaby felt a warm feeling spread from his tummy all the way to his giant paws. Without even thinking about it, he reached down and wrapped his big arms gently around his little friend.

Barnaby smiled a big, toothy smile. He hadn't forgotten how to hug at all! He just needed a good friend to remind him how it started. From that day on, Barnaby's hugs were even better than before, because he knew that the best hugs always start from the heart.
