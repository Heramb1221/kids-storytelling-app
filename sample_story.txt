Once upon a time, in a garden full of sunflowers, there lived a small turtle named Pip.

Pip was the slowest animal in the whole garden, and the other animals often raced past him without a second glance. But Pip did not mind. He liked to notice things that others missed: the way morning dew sparkled on spider webs, and the way bees hummed a different tune every day.

One afternoon, a great storm rolled in. The wind grew wild, and the little rabbits and mice scurried, unable to find their way home. Pip tucked himself into his shell and waited calmly, the way he always did.

When the storm passed, Pip poked his head out and saw the frightened animals hiding under a bush, lost and shivering. Slowly, carefully, Pip led them home, step by patient step, using the paths he had come to know so well from his many slow years of exploring.

From that day on, the animals in the garden learned that being slow was not something to laugh at. Sometimes, the slowest friend knows the way best of all.

The end.
